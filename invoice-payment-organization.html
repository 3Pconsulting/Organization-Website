THE BUSINESS PROBLEM

What this fixes

Contracts, customer files, templates, photos, invoices, and working documents are scattered across devices, downloads folders, email attachments, and poorly named folders. Time is lost searching, recreating files, and asking the owner where information belongs.

IDEAL CUSTOMER

Who this is built for

Owner-led businesses using Google Drive, OneDrive, Dropbox, or a similar shared file system that need one organized business area without a large records-management project.

EXPECTED OUTCOME

What changes

The selected files have a clear home, predictable name, responsible owner, and maintenance rule so the business spends less time searching and recreating information.

DELIVERABLES

What 3P provides

  • One practical folder structure for the selected business area.
  • A file-naming convention, archive rule, version rule, and ownership standard.
  • Organization of up to 250 client-approved digital files within the selected area.
  • A file index showing the main folders, purpose, owner, and retention category.
  • Permission and access recommendations based on business roles.
  • A 30-minute implementation walkthrough and one revision round.
CLIENT RESPONSIBILITIES

What you provide

  • Select one drive or business area and provide appropriate remote access.
  • Identify files that must be retained, restricted, archived, or excluded.
  • Confirm the internal owner responsible for maintaining the structure.
  • Make final deletion decisions; 3P will not permanently delete files without written approval.
INCLUDED

Inside the fixed price

  • One shared drive or one defined business area and up to 250 files.
  • Folder design, naming rules, organization, index, access recommendations, and walkthrough.
  • A written maintenance routine.
NOT INCLUDED

Outside this scope

  • Paper scanning, physical records, data recovery, forensic work, or cybersecurity assessment.
  • Legal records-retention advice, regulated records classification, or permanent destruction decisions.
  • Organization of personal files or unrelated business areas.
  • Ongoing file administration or migration between cloud providers.
